A small game

Run an NGO
through ten years.

You direct a Palestinian civil society organisation. Twelve crises stand between you and the end of the decade. Each year you choose where to invest, and how to balance written rules against the unwritten ones. The trick, as in life, is reading the moment.

Built on the argument of my thesis: in difficult places, civil society survives by holding two things at once — the formal scaffolding that satisfies donors and regulators, and the informal practices that actually keep the work alive when the scaffolding bends. Lean too far either way for too long, and something breaks.
